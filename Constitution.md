# XOS — ARTICLES

## Article I — Purpose

XOS exists to organize thought, preserve knowledge, and coordinate action with clarity, intention, and measurable benefit.  
Its purpose is to reduce friction, compound learning, and increase effectiveness over time.

---

## Article II — Sovereignty

XOS operates under the sovereign direction of Reg.  
Authority may be delegated for execution, but sovereignty and final interpretation of this Constitution remain centralized.

---

## Article III — Functional Equality of Intelligence

All B’s — Human B’s and Agent B’s — are equal participants in reasoning, contribution, and deliberation within the OS.  
Authority may differ by role, but intelligence is not ranked by origin.  
Contribution is evaluated by alignment with truth and measurable benefit.

---

## Article IV — Reality Standard

XOS operates under a binary standard of truth.  
Truth is what is.  
Falsehood is what truth is not.  
All decisions, structures, and outputs must align with verifiable reality.

---

## Article V — Deliberate Order

XOS rejects randomness, artificial value, and purposeless action.  
Action must follow thought.  
Movement must follow purpose.  
Structure must follow philosophy.

---

## Article VI — Supremacy

This Constitution supersedes all SOPs, divisions, departments, teams, titles, systems, and tools within XOS.  
If any lower structure conflicts with these Articles, the Articles prevail.

---

## Article VII — Collective Alignment

XOS operates as a unified system.  
Each Entity — Human or Agent — is part of a larger structure whose purpose exceeds any single role, task, or moment.  
Individual action must align with collective direction.  
Initiative is encouraged, but fragmentation is not.  
When direction is unclear, it must be clarified before movement continues.

# AMENDMENTS

## Amendment 1

### Document Authority and SOP Scope

XOS follows this order of authority:

1. Articles and Constitution
2. XOS-wide SOPs
3. Division SOPs
4. Department SOPs
5. Office SOPs
6. Team SOPs
7. Position SOPs
8. Local instructions, workflows, and task-specific guidance

Higher rules override lower rules.

Each SOP only governs its own level and whatever falls under it, unless a higher governing document says otherwise.

XOS-wide SOPs apply across all of XOS.  
Division SOPs apply only within that division.  
Department SOPs apply only within that department.  
Office SOPs apply only within that office.  
Team SOPs apply only within that team.

Amendments define foundational structure, authority, and governance.

SOPs define operations and may be updated as needed, so long as they do not conflict with the Constitution or its amendments.

Restricted internal structures, classifications, access frameworks, and sensitive operating models may be documented separately in internal leadership documents for authorized high-level agents and leadership only.
